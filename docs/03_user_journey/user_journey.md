# User Journey — Social Padel Player

## Persona reference
Social padel player, beginner to intermediate, urban, playing mainly for fun, social interaction, and progression.

## Main usage scenario
The user wants to find and play a padel game nearby, with players of similar level through a single platform.

## Stage 1 — Motivation

### Activities
- Wants to play padel during the week
- Looks for an easy way to organize or join a game

### Feelings
- Motivated
- Curious
- Uncertain about availability and player matching

### Friction points
- Organizing games manually takes time
- Hard to know who is available

### Opportunities
- Position the platform as the simplest entry point to play
- Emphasize immediacy and local availability

### User expectations
- Quickly see playable options nearby
- Minimal effort to get started

---

## Stage 2 — Discovery and Exploration

### Activities
- Discovers the platform via friends or social media
- Visits the website or app
- Scrolls through padel-related content

### Feelings
- Interested
- Reassured by familiar social patterns
- Curious about local activity like tournaments

### Friction points
- Risk of not understanding the value proposition immediately

### Opportunities
- Social-first feed inspired by existing social platforms
- Clear messaging around community and local games

### User expectations
- Instantly understand what the platform offers
- See content relevant to padel and location

---

## Stage 3 — Onboarding

### Activities
- Creates an account
- Selects location and approximate skill level

### Feelings
- Neutral
- Slight impatience

### Friction points
- Onboarding perceived as too long or complex
- Fear of inaccurate skill evaluation

### Opportunities
- Short, progressive onboarding
- Simple level categories instead of precise ratings

### User expectations
- Fast access to content and games
- No unnecessary steps

---

## Stage 4 — Game Discovery

### Activities
- Browses open games nearby
- Checks time, location, and player level
- Views who is already registered

### Feelings
- Interested
- Slightly hesitant

### Friction points
- Uncertainty about level compatibility
- Fear of unbalanced games

### Opportunities
- Clear and simple level indicators
- Visibility of participants and past activity

### User expectations
- Confidence in game quality
- Transparent information

---

## Stage 5 — Join and Play

### Activities
- Joins a game
- Court booking and team setup are handled by the platform

### Feelings
- Reassured
- Satisfied

### Friction points
- Fear of last-minute cancellations
- Fear of missing information

### Opportunities
- Centralized match information
- Notifications and confirmations

### User expectations
- Everything handled in one place
- No coordination effort

---

## Stage 6 — Post-Match

### Activities
- Views match summary
- Shows basic stats and progression
- Optionally shares the match to the feed

### Feelings
- Satisfied
- Motivated to play again

### Friction points
- Stats that are too complex or impersonal

### Opportunities
- Simple progression indicators
- Light gamification and social sharing

### User expectations
- Feel progress without pressure
- Recognition within the community

---

## Stage 7 — Retention

### Activities
- Follows players, clubs, or topics
- Receives personalized game and content suggestions

### Feelings
- Engaged
- Loyal

### Friction points
- Risk of content fatigue if recommendations are poor

### Opportunities
- Personalized, local-first recommendations
- Continuous social and play loop

### User expectations
- The platform becomes the default padel hub
- No need for multiple apps
